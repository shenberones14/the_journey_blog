BACK TO SCHOOL





Today marked my return to school, a journey laden with emotions and apprehensions. As I walked through the halls, the weight of the past seemed to echo with every step. The first day, once filled with excitement and anticipation, now carried a different kind of burden.

Entering the classroom, I couldn’t shake the feeling of being exposed, vulnerable to the gazes and whispers that might follow. The ordinary chatter around me felt like a distant murmur as my mind grappled with the memories of the trauma that still lingered.

The faces of new classmates, looks friendly and familiar, now seemed like potential sources of judgment. Would they see beyond the scars I carry, or would my presence be defined by the events that unfolded in the shadows?

The teacher’s voice, explaining the syllabus and expectations, became a distant hum as my thoughts circled around a whirlwind of emotions. Anxiety, fear, and a desperate longing for normalcy wove a complex tapestry within me.

I sought refuge in the anonymity of the crowd, hoping to blend in and go unnoticed. Each interaction felt like a delicate dance, a tightrope walk between revealing too much and concealing too little. The ache for understanding and acceptance hung heavy in the air.

As the school day unfolded, I found solace in small moments of kindness—a sympathetic smile, a supportive gesture from a teacher. These fleeting connections became lifelines, hinting at the possibility of a sanctuary within the familiar walls.

The journey has just begun, and the road ahead seems uncertain. But within the pages of this journey, I hope to document not just the challenges but also the moments of resilience and the gradual reclamation of a sense of self.
