HEALING IN TIME’S EMBRACE





In the quiet corridors of my heart, where echoes of pain linger, I find solace in a whispered mantra: “Time will heal me.” It’s like a comforting thought, but I can’t help wondering, “When will I actually heal?”

Imagine life as a journey, and I’m on a path where time is supposed to make things better. However, I don’t know exactly when that will happen, and it leaves me with a lingering question: When will I feel better?

As I go through the ups and downs of my emotions, I see time as a helpful companion, slowly working to untangle the knots of sadness. I keep telling myself, “I don’t know when,” letting time do its thing at its own pace.

The marks of pain, which used to be so noticeable, started to fade away. It’s a bit like a flower slowly opening up. With each new day, I find a bit more strength, even though I still wonder, “When will I fully heal?”

This process isn’t quick, but I’m starting to see positive changes. The question of when things will get better remains, but it’s also a reminder that healing takes time. I’m trusting that, as time goes on, the answer to when I will heal will become clearer. It’s like patiently waiting for the sun to rise after a long night.
